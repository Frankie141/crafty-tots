Edible Paints

Edible paints are safe and fun ideas for toddlers.

Supplies You Will Need

  • A Blender
  • A saucepan/steamer
  • Broccoli, Strawberries and carrots - for the paints
  • Large sheet of white paper

Instructions

  1. Prepare and cook carrots and broccoli.
  2. Blend each vegetable seperatly to make the orange and green paint.
  3. Blend Strawberries to make red paint.
  4. Lay the large sheet of white paper on your toddlers high chair tray or safely seated at a toddler table and chairs
  5. Dont forget this can get messy so bath time immediately after may be required.

Edible Playdough

A safe, non-toxic recipe for Edible Playdough

Supplies You Will Need

  • 50g Icing Sugar Approximately 6 Tbsp
  • 200g Marshmallows
  • Food Colouring Paste

Instructions

  1. Place the Marshmallows in a microwave bowl.
  2. Put in the microwave and melt in 10 second bursts.
  3. Stir between each burst - it should take approximately 40 seconds to melt in the microwave.
  4. Add your sugar a spoon at a time and mix in.
  5. When your mixture becomes a sticky test, than remove from the bowl and place on a clean surface with icing sugar.
  6. Add more of the sugar into the center of the dough and fold in.
  7. Keep adding, folding and kneading lightly dusted surface until you have some play dough.

No-Mess Painting

A mess free activity for your little ones

Supplies

  • A large clear ziplock bag
  • Child-friendly, non-toxic paints

Instructions

  1. Make sure you take the majority of the air out of the ziplock bags.
  2. Fill the ziplock bag with a number of different colour paints. You will need enough space to allow the paint to move around freely.
  3. Place the ziplock bag on the tray of the highchair in front of your toddler.
  4. You may find it helpful to tape the ziplock bag to the tray to stop your little throwing it on the floor.
